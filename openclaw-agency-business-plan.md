# OpenClaw Implementation & Automation Agency – Business Plan

## 1. Core Opportunity

**Offer:** OpenClaw Implementation & Automation Agency

**Target buyer:**
- Founders and small teams with existing tools and workflows (email, CRM, Slack, calendars, etc.)
- Agencies / SaaS teams that want a durable agent layer instead of scattered scripts and Zaps

**Painful problems:**
- Lots of AI demos, very few agents doing real work inside the business
- Fragile “glue” automation (Zaps, scripts, Make) that nobody fully owns
- "We should use AI for this" comes up in meetings but never turns into shipped systems

**Why this is attractive:**
- High urgency around "doing something with AI" now
- Strong willingness to pay for durable, safe automation
- Highly leverageable: you can standardize architectures, skills, and workflows over time

---

## 2. Business Blueprint (Product Architecture)

### Core Offer – OpenClaw Revenue & Ops Engine

A 6–8 week done-with-you / done-for-you engagement to:
- Map top leverage use cases
- Design 1–3 OpenClaw agents with clear roles and guardrails
- Implement them in the client’s OpenClaw environment
- Launch, monitor, and tune them against real workflows

### Unique Positioning
- OpenClaw specialist (agent framework), not a generic AI automation shop
- Focus on **agents that live inside the stack** with logging, monitoring, and safety
- Clear documentation and architecture diagrams so clients aren’t dependent on "mystery glue"

### Pricing & Offers

- **Flagship Implementation:**
  - 6–8 weeks, 1–3 agents
  - Pricing target: **$4,000–$7,500** per project

- **OpenClaw Quickstart:**
  - Single high-impact agent (e.g., lead intake, support triage, reporting)
  - Pricing target: **$1,500–$2,500**

- **AI Ops Retainer (Optional):**
  - Ongoing improvements, new workflows, monitoring, emergency fixes
  - Pricing target: **$1,000–$3,000/month**

### Fulfillment Model

- Remote, hybrid done-with-you / done-for-you
- Phases:
  1. Discovery & use case mapping
  2. Design & architecture
  3. Implementation in OpenClaw
  4. Launch, monitoring, tuning
- Deliverables: running agents, architecture map, SOPs, backlog of future agents

### Revenue Model

- Project-based fees + optional recurring retainers
- Over time, reuse patterns to reduce time per project and increase effective hourly rate

## 3. Sales Page – Structure & Messaging

### Hero

- Eyebrow: `OPENCLAW IMPLEMENTATION • DONE-WITH-YOU & DONE-FOR-YOU`
- Headline: `Turn “We Should Use AI” Into Agents That Actually Run Parts of Your Business`
- Subheadline: Emphasize "no more demos" and real agents in their stack
- Hero elements (in order):
  1. Eyebrow
  2. Headline (with highlight)
  3. Subheadline
  4. Video explainer (3–5 min)
  5. CTA button: **“Book Your OpenClaw Implementation Call”**

### Sections

1. **Problem:** AI demos vs real agents, glue-code mess, "someday" backlog
2. **Solution/Mechanism:** OpenClaw agents as an ops layer; your role as specialist implementer
3. **Timeline:** 4 phases – Map → Design → Implement → Tune (6–8 weeks)
4. **Comparison:** Generic AI automation vs OpenClaw specialist implementation
5. **Features & Benefits:** Tangible deliverables + business outcomes
6. **Offer & Pricing:** Flagship implementation, Quickstart, optional retainer
7. **Guarantee:** Clarity + at least one production agent and architecture, or you keep working / adjust fee
8. **FAQ:** Non-technical buyers, safety, measurement, tools, etc.
9. **Final CTA:** Reinforce outcome and invite to book call

## 5. Traffic & Conversion Assets

### Organic

- Platforms: X, LinkedIn, email list as baseline
- Assets:
  - 1 primary launch post
  - 5 follow-up posts
  - 10 engagement hooks (questions, prompts)
  - 5 authority posts about automation design, ownership, logging, etc.

- Launch cadence (example):
  - 3–5 posts/week on X + LinkedIn
  - 2–3 emails/week during launch (over 2–3 weeks)

### Email Sequence (7 Emails)

1. Announcement – open spots for OpenClaw implementations
2. Problem – cost of “we’ll automate this later”
3. Solution – what an OpenClaw implementation actually looks like
4. Mechanism – agents vs bots; why OpenClaw
5. Objections – security, reliability, technical skill, replacement fears
6. Gentle urgency – opportunity cost / “if not now, when?”
7. Final call – last reminder for this round

### Paid Ads

- Campaign types:
  - Cold – awareness (OpenClaw + agents messaging)
  - Warm – visitors and video viewers
  - Retargeting – sales page visitors who didn’t book

- Budget (starting):
  - Cold: $20–$40/day
  - Warm: $10–$20/day
  - Retargeting: $5–$15/day

- Creatives:
  - 5 short hooks
  - 3 long-form ads
  - 2 retargeting ads
  - 10 headline variations

### Tracking

- Events: page view, CTA click, call booked, (optionally client won)
- Pixels: Meta/LinkedIn pixels on sales + thank-you pages; basic event mapping

## 7. Optimization & Scale

### Optimization Levers

1. **Landing Page Conversion:**
   - Test hero variants and CTA framing
   - Add specificity near CTA (what happens on the call)

2. **Call Quality:**
   - Qualifying bullets and a short pre-call form

3. **Offer Framing:**
   - Clear ladder from Quickstart → Flagship → Retainer

### Scaling Strategies

1. Deepen one main channel (e.g., LinkedIn + Meta ads) until predictable
2. Productize by segment: "OpenClaw for Agencies", "OpenClaw for Course Creators", etc.
3. Standardize SOPs and bring in help for implementation while you own architecture and client strategy

### Long-Term Brand Expansion

- Build an **“OpenClaw Ops Lab”**:
  - Education + community for teams running agents in production
  - Template + skill library
  - Acts as front-end for services and standalone revenue stream

### Revenue Targets & Thresholds

- Baseline target:
  - 2 implementation clients/month @ ~$5k → ~$10k/month project revenue
  - 1–2 retainers @ $2k/month → +$2–4k MRR accumulating

- Scale up when:
  - You can consistently close ~2 clients/month without delivery strain
  - You have repeatable implementation patterns
