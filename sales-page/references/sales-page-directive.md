# Sales Page Directive Reference

Use this file as style guidance for long-form conversion pages.

## Core Principle

Build a persuasive sales argument with psychological completeness.
Do not shorten prematurely. Do not replace specificity with hype.
Optimize for clarity, credibility, momentum, and conversion depth.

## Recommended Persuasion Elements

### 1. Outcome Anchoring

Translate abstract benefits into tangible outcomes.
Examples:
- launch more offers in less time
- validate ideas before investing heavily
- publish faster with less friction
- turn attention into a sellable asset

Only use measurable claims when the user provides support for them.

### 2. Proof Layer

Use the strongest available proof, in this order:
1. real results and case studies
2. process demonstrations
3. concrete timelines
4. logic-based validation

Never invent:
- testimonials
- screenshots
- revenue numbers
- conversion rates
- customer outcomes

### 3. Objection Handling

Address the most likely blockers:
- time
- technical complexity
- skepticism
- prior failed purchases
- lack of audience
- fear of not finishing
- uncertainty about niche or offer fit

Use logic, reassurance, and specificity.

### 4. Identity Shift

Show the buyer’s upgraded role in believable terms.
Examples:
- operator
- builder
- decisive founder
- disciplined creator
- early mover

Anchor identity language to the actual transformation created by the offer.

### 5. Future Visualization

Use concrete scenes, not vague fantasy.
Examples:
- seeing the finished sales asset live
- launching while others are still planning
- reviewing a clean published funnel instead of a half-finished draft

### 6. Differentiation

Explain what the offer is not, then explain what makes it distinct.
This helps reduce category confusion and generic-AI skepticism.

### 7. Urgency

Use only real urgency:
- actual deadlines
- genuine founder pricing
- real bonus expiry
- real seat or rollout limits

No fake scarcity.

### 8. CTA Stack

At the close, make sure the buyer understands:
- what they get
- what happens right after purchase
- what risk is reduced
- why acting now makes sense

## Tone Guidance

Preferred tone:
- calm authority
- sharp but controlled
- specific
- persuasive without melodrama
- premium rather than noisy
