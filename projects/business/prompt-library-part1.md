## 25 EMAIL MARKETING PROMPTS

### Welcome Sequences

**PROMPT 1: Welcome Email #1**
```
You are a conversion copywriter with 10 years of experience writing welcome emails for [INDUSTRY]. Write the first email in a 5-part welcome sequence for [PRODUCT/SERVICE]. This email should: 1) Thank them for joining, 2) Set expectations for what's coming, 3) Deliver immediate value with [SPECIFIC TIP/RESOURCE], 4) Include a soft CTA to [ACTION]. Tone: [FRIENDLY/PROFESSIONAL/ENTHUSIASTIC]. Length: 150-200 words.
```

**PROMPT 2: Welcome Email #2 - Origin Story**
```
Write the second email in a welcome sequence. This email should share the origin story of [BRAND/COMPANY]: 1) The problem the founder faced, 2) The moment of insight, 3) The struggle to solve it, 4) The breakthrough. Make it personal and relatable. Include a lesson the reader can apply. Tone: conversational and authentic. Length: 200-250 words.
```

**PROMPT 3: Welcome Email #3 - Value Proof**
```
Write the third welcome email that demonstrates value. Include: 1) A specific case study or result, 2) The strategy or method used, 3) Why it works, 4) How the reader can apply it. Use specific numbers and details. Include social proof. Tone: helpful and authoritative. Length: 200-250 words.
```

**PROMPT 4: Welcome Email #4 - Overcome Objections**
```
Write the fourth welcome email addressing common objections: 1) Acknowledge the objection ([PRICE/TIME/RESULTS]), 2) Reframe it with a story or example, 3) Provide proof it works, 4) Offer a guarantee or risk reversal. Make the reader feel understood, not sold to. Tone: empathetic and confident. Length: 200-250 words.
```

**PROMPT 5: Welcome Email #5 - Sales CTA**
```
Write the final welcome email with a clear sales CTA: 1) Recap the value received so far, 2) Present the offer ([PRODUCT/SERVICE]), 3) List 3-5 specific benefits, 4) Include price and guarantee, 5) Strong CTA with urgency. Make it feel like a natural next step, not a hard sell. Tone: confident and helpful. Length: 250-300 words.
```

### Email Frameworks

**PROMPT 21: PAS Framework Email**
```
Write an email using the PAS framework (Problem-Agitate-Solution): 1) Problem: Identify [SPECIFIC PAIN POINT], 2) Agitate: Make the pain feel urgent and real, 3) Solution: Present [PRODUCT/SERVICE] as the answer, 4) CTA: Clear next step. Make the agitation section emotional and specific. Tone: empathetic then confident. Length: 250-300 words.
```

**PROMPT 22: AIDA Framework Email**
```
Write an email using the AIDA framework (Attention-Interest-Desire-Action): 1) Attention: Bold statement or question, 2) Interest: Why they should care, 3) Desire: Benefits and proof, 4) Action: Strong CTA. Each section should flow naturally to the next. Tone: persuasive. Length: 250-300 words.
```

**PROMPT 23: Story-Based Email**
```
Write a story-based email using the Hero's Journey: 1) Ordinary world (the problem), 2) Call to adventure (the opportunity), 3) Challenges faced, 4) The transformation, 5) Return with wisdom (the lesson). Use [CUSTOMER/STORY] as the hero. Make it relatable and inspiring. Tone: narrative-driven. Length: 300-400 words.
```

**PROMPT 24: Comparison Email**
```
Write a comparison email showing [YOUR SOLUTION] vs [ALTERNATIVE/COMPETITOR]. Include: 1) Acknowledge the alternative (don't bash it), 2) Where it falls short for [SPECIFIC USE CASE], 3) How your solution is different, 4) Side-by-side comparison (if applicable), 5) Who each is best for, 6) CTA for your solution. Be fair but persuasive. Tone: honest and helpful. Length: 300-400 words.
```

**PROMPT 25: Value-First Email**
```
Write a value-first email that delivers a complete mini-lesson on [TOPIC]. Include: 1) Hook that promises value, 2) The concept explained simply, 3) Step-by-step how to implement, 4) Example or case study, 5) Common mistakes to avoid, 6) Soft CTA to go deeper. Make it so valuable they could charge for it. Tone: generous and expert. Length: 400-500 words.
```
